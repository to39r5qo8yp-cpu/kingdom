# Pre-Sell Validation Script

Based on Dan Martell's framework: "When you ask for advice instead of money, people open up."

---

## Philosophy

**The Goal:** Validate demand before building. Get paid before delivering.

**The Question:** "What's hard in your business that could be automated with AI?"

**The Signal:** They describe the pain in detail, ask about pricing, or want to see it working.

---

## Day 1: Draft Your One-Page Offer

### Offer Structure (Dan's Framework)

```markdown
# [OFFER NAME]

## Outcome
One specific result they get

## Mechanism
How you deliver it (3-5 steps)

## Timeline
How long until delivered

## Price
50% deposit, 50% on delivery

## Scarcity
Limited spots/time-limited

## Guarantee
What happens if you fail
```

### Example One-Pager

```markdown
# Email Triage System

## Outcome
Zero inbox overwhelm. Every email categorized, prioritized,
and actionable — without reading a single one.

## Mechanism
1. Connect to your Gmail/Outlook
2. AI categorizes by your rules
3. Daily digest to your channel
4. Priority alerts for urgent items

## Timeline
5 business days

## Price
$1,500 (50% deposit)

## Scarcity
5 founding client spots this month

## Guarantee
If accuracy < 80% by Day 5, second 50% free
```

## Day 2: Build Your Prospect List

### Target: 100 Prospects

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Industry Facebook groups
- Local business directories
- Your existing network
- Conference attendee lists

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Business owner or decision-maker
- Has the problem you solve
- Has budget to spend ($1K-5K minimum)
- Can say yes quickly

### AI-Powered Prospect Building

```
Prompt: "Show me 20 boring industries with high average
deal sizes where operations are still manual, ranked by
AI automation opportunity."
```

## Day 3-4: Outreach Scripts

### Cold Call Script (2 min)

```
"Hi [Name], I'm [Your name]. I help [industry] businesses
automate [specific pain point] with AI.

I noticed [observation about their business].

I'm building an [offer name] that [outcome] —
would you be open to a 15-minute call to tell me
what's hard about [problem] in your business?

I'm not selling anything today — just gathering
advice from smart operators like you."
```

### Cold Email Script

```
Subject: Quick question about [pain point]

Hi [Name],

I'm building an AI automation that helps [industry]
businesses [outcome] — and I'm looking for smart
operators to give me feedback.

Would you have 15 minutes to share what's hard about
[problem] in your business?

No pitch, just advice. In exchange, I'll share what's
working for others in [industry].

[Your name]

P.S. I'm only talking to 10 people this week, so
if this timing works, let me know.
```

### LinkedIn DM Script

```
Hi [Name], I noticed you're in [industry].

I'm building an AI tool that [outcome], and I'm
looking for 5 [industry] owners to give me brutal
feedback on whether this would actually help.

Mind if I ask: what's the most frustrating part of
[problem] in your business?

No sales pitch — just looking for advice from
someone who knows the space.
```

## Day 5: Book Calls and Close

### The Validation Call (15 min)

**Opening (1 min):**
```
"Thanks for taking the call. As I mentioned, I'm not
selling anything today. I'm building [offer name] and
want to make sure I'm solving a real problem.

Before I tell you about it, can I ask — what's the
hardest part about [problem] for you right now?"
```

**Discovery (7 min):**
- Let them talk (80% them, 20% you)
- Ask follow-ups: "Tell me more about that"
- Quantify: "How much time/money does that cost?"
- Emotional: "How does that make you feel?"

**Present (5 min):**
``"Based on what you shared, here's what I'm building..."

- Walk through the offer
- Connect each feature to their pain
- Show the timeline and guarantee

**The Ask (2 min):**
```
"I'm opening 5 founding client spots this month at
$1,500. You'd get [everything above] plus 30 days
of support.

Would this be valuable enough to invest in if I
can deliver on the promise?"
```

**Validation Signals:**
| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Describes pain in detail | Good — real problem |
| Asks about price | Buying signal |
| Wants to see it working | Strong buying signal |
| Says "interesting" and moves on | Weak — may not be a fit |
| Negotiates timing | Buying signal |
| Asks about guarantee | Near close |

## Handling Objections

### "I need to think about it"
```
"Totally understand. What specifically would you
need to see to feel confident this would work for
your business?"
```

### "That seems expensive"
```
"Fair point. Let me ask — how much time/money are
you losing each month to [problem]? What would it
be worth to solve that permanently?"
```

### "I can probably build this myself"
```
"You absolutely could. The question is whether
building it yourself is the best use of your time.
What's your hourly rate? [Do the math]"
```

### "I need to talk to my partner"
```
"Of course. When would be a good time to follow up?
Would it help if I sent you a one-page summary to
share with them?"
```

## The Pre-Sell Close

**If they're ready:**
```
"Great, let me get you the intake form and we'll
schedule your discovery call for [Day]. I'll send
over the invoice for the deposit — you pay the
rest when it's delivered and working."
```

**If they need follow-up:**
```
"I'll send you a summary and we can reconnect on
[Date]. Sound good?"

[Send within 24 hours]
```
